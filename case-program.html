Case Study

Building Success: A Program Onboarding Experience

Designing a learner success system that improved ownership, accountability, stakeholder alignment, and performance expectations.

Behavior Change · Program Development
← Return to Portfolio
AudienceMiddle school choir learners & families
TimelineFull academic year
RoleProgram designer & facilitator
DeliverablesHandbook, presentations, leadership pathways

Learning Challenge

The Problem

Learners entered the program with inconsistent expectations, limited ownership, and behaviors that created barriers to high-quality performance. Families also needed clearer communication around attendance, participation, and performance requirements. The challenge was not simply to explain policies; it was to create behavior change and establish a culture of accountability.

Solution

What I Designed

I developed a comprehensive onboarding and learner success system that combined a handbook, leadership pathways, parent-facing orientation materials, acknowledgement forms, and evolving performance expectations into one cohesive structure. The system clarified what success looked like while giving learners meaningful ways to contribute to the program.

Design decisions

The Choices Behind the Work

Clear Expectations

The handbook was designed as a living performance support resource, not a static policy document.

Learner Ownership

Management team and Maestro roles created visible leadership pathways and incentives for positive behavior.

Stakeholder Alignment

A parent presentation reduced information overload and reinforced the most critical expectations in a more accessible format.

Results & impact

What Changed

  • Greater learner ownership and participation
  • Increased interest in leadership opportunities
  • More consistent accountability around performance expectations
  • Reduced preventable confusion with families

Reflection

The strongest design decision was treating the onboarding system as a living document. As learner needs changed, the system evolved to address new performance gaps, behavioral patterns, and stakeholder needs.
