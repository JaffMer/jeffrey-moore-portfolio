Case Study

Rehearsal to Concert: A Performance-Based Learning Experience

Designing a scaffolded learning journey that moved learners from foundational skill acquisition to authentic performance and artistic application.

Learning Transfer · Assessment · Performance Support
← Return to Portfolio
AudienceMiddle school choir learners
TimelineSemester-long concert cycle
RoleLearning designer, facilitator, coach
DeliverablesRubrics, forms, practice tracks, reflection tools

Learning Challenge

The Problem

Learners demonstrated inconsistent retention and transfer of musical skills. While students could often perform isolated tasks during rehearsal, applying those skills confidently in authentic performance settings required a more intentional learning system.

Solution

What I Designed

I designed a performance-based learning experience that combined scaffolded rehearsal, formative assessment, practice resources, real-time coaching, authentic performance, and post-performance reflection. The concert became the final demonstration of learning rather than the only learning event.

Design decisions

The Choices Behind the Work

Practice Before Theory

Learners experienced and practiced concepts before connecting them to formal notation and terminology.

Formative Feedback Loop

Skills checks, self-assessment, coaching, and individualized feedback informed future rehearsals.

Authentic Assessment

Concerts and performances served as real-world demonstrations of skill application and transfer.

Results & impact

What Changed

  • Rehearsals shifted from note accuracy to higher-order interpretation, phrasing, historical context, and expression
  • Learners demonstrated increased confidence and performance readiness
  • Reflection activities extended learning beyond the concert
  • Assessment results informed future instruction

Reflection

The most important insight was that authentic performance depends on more than final preparation. It requires recurring feedback, accessible performance supports, and opportunities for learners to reflect on how their skills are developing over time.
